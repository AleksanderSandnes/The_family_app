# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

adaptive

## Users

Families — parents and kids in one household, each on their own phone. Sessions are short, glanceable, in-between moments: checking the list at the store, planning dinner, seeing who's where, wishing someone happy birthday. Every age in the family, from a ten-year-old to a grandparent, must be able to use it without instruction.

## Product Purpose

One shared space for everything a family coordinates: shopping, meals, calendar, birthdays, wishlists, chat, and location. Two first-class native apps (Jetpack Compose on Android, SwiftUI on iOS) over one shared Supabase backend. Success is a polished public Play Store / App Store release that acquires and retains real families beyond the author's own.

## Positioning

One shared home for the family — everything a household coordinates lives in one private space, instead of six scattered apps.

## Brand Personality

Warm, premium, calm. The app feels like a well-crafted home: inviting indigo/violet color, soft Liquid Glass surfaces, unhurried motion. Copy is friendly and plain-spoken (English and Norwegian Bokmål), never corporate and never cutesy.

## Anti-references

- **Kids' app / cartoon** — no mascots, bubbly primary colors, or toy-like chrome; adults must feel this is a serious, premium tool.
- **Generic Material template** — must never read as a default Material 3 scaffold; the shared Liquid Glass identity stays distinct on both platforms.
- **Corporate productivity tool** — not Jira-for-families; no dense enterprise chrome or gray utilitarianism.

## Design Principles

1. **Calm over clamor** — family logistics are already stressful; the app absorbs stress rather than adding it. Quiet surfaces, restrained motion, no urgency theater.
2. **Glanceable first** — every screen answers its question in a moment. The user is mid-errand; the answer (what's for dinner, what's left to buy, who's where) leads, and everything else follows.
3. **One home, many rooms** — features feel like rooms of the same house, not separate apps. One component vocabulary, one glass language, per-feature accent colors for orientation only.
4. **Effortless for every age** — a kid and a grandparent both succeed on the first try. Standard platform affordances over invented ones; clarity over density.
5. **Premium earns trust** — store-release polish everywhere: crafted empty, loading, and error states; both themes; both languages; both platforms at parity.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as the working bar for a public store release: AA contrast in both light and dark themes, respect for system font scaling and reduced-motion settings, and full TalkBack / VoiceOver labels on interactive elements. Full English and Norwegian Bokmål localization with an in-app language switch.
